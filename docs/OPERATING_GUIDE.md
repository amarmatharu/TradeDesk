# How To Actually Run This — one-page operating guide

> The plain-language playbook for running "THE PLAN" (the unified core-satellite
> engine) with real money. Read the caveats at the bottom first.

---

## What this is, in one sentence
Once a week, the app tells you what % of your money should be in **stocks** vs
**bonds** (sized by how calm or turbulent the market is, and switched fully to
bonds if the market is in a downtrend). You just match your account to that. That's it.

---

## Step 1 — The funds you actually buy
You only need **three** low-cost ETFs. Buy them in a normal brokerage or IRA:

| Role in THE PLAN | Buy this (ETF) | Alternatives |
|---|---|---|
| **US stocks (SPY)** | **VOO** (or SPY, IVV) | any S&P 500 / total-US fund |
| **International stocks (EFA)** | **IEFA** (or EFA, VEA) | any developed-intl fund |
| **Bonds (AGG)** | **BND** (or AGG) | total-bond / short-treasury (BIL) |

*(VOO/IEFA/BND are the cheapest versions of the same things the model uses. When
THE PLAN says "SPY," buy VOO; when it says "EFA," buy IEFA; "AGG bonds" = BND.)*

---

## Step 2 — The weekly routine (5 minutes, once a week)
1. Pick a fixed day — say **Friday after close** or **Sunday**. Same day every week.
2. Open the app → **Health tab** → the gold **"THE PLAN"** card.
3. Read the **CORE allocation**, e.g. `SPY 4.8% · AGG bonds 95.2%`.
4. Make your account match those percentages (see Step 3).
5. Done. Ignore the market until next week.

**That's the whole job.** No charts, no news, no predictions. The discipline of
*doing only this and nothing else* is where the edge actually lives.

---

## Step 3 — How to rebalance (match your account to the targets)
Say your portfolio is **$10,000** and THE PLAN says **SPY 4.8% / bonds 95.2%**:
- Target: **$480 in VOO**, **$9,520 in BND**.
- Compare to what you hold now; buy/sell the difference. Round to whole shares — no need to be exact to the dollar.
- If a target moved only a little from last week (a few %), **don't bother trading** — only rebalance when it moves ~5%+ . This saves costs and taxes.

**Regime flip = the important one.** If the card flips **RISK-ON → DEFENSIVE**
(GEM crash protection turns on), move **everything to BND**. If it flips back to
RISK-ON, follow the new stock %. These flips are rare (a few times a decade) and
are the whole point — they're what dodge the −55% crashes.

---

## Step 4 — The satellite (agent news/insider signals)
**Recommended: skip it entirely.** Our own testing showed these signals have **no
proven edge**, so the honest default is 0%.

If you want to dabble anyway, treat it as **entertainment money, not investing**:
- Cap the *whole* satellite at **≤10% of your account**, and any single trade at **~2%**.
- Only act on signals the card shows as high-conviction, and let the Risk Guard's
  limits stand. When one closes, the Journal logs the lesson.
- Never, ever fund the satellite by shrinking the core. If it goes to zero, shrug.

---

## Step 5 — The rules that make it work (don't skip these)
1. **Get off margin/leverage first.** Leverage turns normal dips into wipeouts.
   This is step zero, before anything else.
2. **Don't override the card.** The moment you "have a feeling" and deviate, you've
   turned a validated system back into gambling. The rules beat your gut — that was
   the whole finding.
3. **Only money you won't touch for 5+ years.** This compounds over cycles, not weeks.
4. **Use a tax-advantaged account (IRA) if you can** — the weekly bond/stock shifts
   create taxable events in a regular account. In an IRA, they don't.
5. **Expect to trail in booming bull markets.** When stocks scream up, you'll hold
   some bonds and lag a pure stock portfolio. That's the *price of the insurance*,
   and you pay it happily the year the market drops 50% and you don't.

## What to realistically expect
- **Roughly market-like returns over full cycles, with far smaller crashes**
  (in testing: ~1/3 the worst-case drawdown). Smoother ride, similar destination.
- **Not** a get-rich engine, **not** beating the market on raw return (nothing does).
- Its real value is behavioral: a ride calm enough that **you actually stick with it**
  instead of panic-selling the bottom — which is where most people lose.

## ⚠️ Caveats (read these)
- This is an **educational tool built from our own research, not financial advice.**
  I'm an AI, not a licensed advisor. For decisions about your real finances, taxes,
  and goals, talk to a **fee-only fiduciary** (paid by you, not by commissions).
- **Paper-trade / track it for a few months first** to build trust and learn the routine.
- The app's numbers are **recommendations only — it never places an order.** You are
  always the one who decides and executes.
- Start small. Keep an emergency cash cushion completely separate from any of this.
